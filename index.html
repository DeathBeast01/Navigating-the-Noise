The Real Deal: Facts Over Fiction

Understand the truth about the most common substances:

  • Tobacco/Vaping: Nicotine dependence, long-term health risks, and the financial burden.
  • Alcohol: The legal and social risks in Indian context, and the reality of liver and neurological damage.
  • Cannabis (Marijuana): Fact-checking its harmless image and discussing mental health risks, including potential psychosis.

Stress & Coping: Better Ways to Handle Pressure

Practical, healthy ways to tackle tough times without substances:

  • Academic Stress: Time management, mindful study breaks, and talking to mentors.
  • Peer Pressure: Strategies for saying 'No' confidently and finding genuine friends.
  • Relationship Management: Dealing with breakups, family conflict, and loneliness constructively.

Warning Signs: Beyond the Report Card

Focus on behavioral and emotional shifts:

  • Sudden Mood Swings: Unusual irritability, anxiety, or depression.
  • Loss of Interest: Dropping hobbies or activities they once enjoyed.
  • Changes in Sleep/Eating: Irregular patterns, unexplained weight loss or gain.
  • Secretive Behavior: Unexplained disappearances or lying about whereabouts.

How to Talk: Non-Judgmental Scripts

Focus on a supportive, non-critical approach. (Addressing communication barriers)

Instead of: "Why are you ruining your life? You need to stop now!"
Try: "I'm worried about you. I've noticed [specific change in behavior]. Can we talk about what's going on?"
Instead of: "Don't you care about your future?"
Try: "You seem stressed/sad. I'm here to support you in finding a better way to cope."

Family Support Self-Help Groups (SHGs)

Connecting with other parents facing similar challenges.

  • Find a Group: Many Integrated Rehabilitation Centres for Addicts (IRCAs) supported by MoSJE facilitate family support meetings and SHGs.
  • Connect Locally: Reach out to your nearest IRCA (listed in Resources) for information on local support networks.
  • Online Forums: Seek out verified online forums dedicated to parents of youth dealing with substance abuse.

Facilities supported by the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment (MoSJE) under the Nasha Mukt Bharat Abhiyaan.

Integrated Rehabilitation Centres for Addicts (IRCAs)

IRCAs provide comprehensive services including preventive education, motivational counseling, detoxification/de-addiction, aftercare, and social re-integration.

üîó Action Step: To find the nearest center, you must check the official directory. Call 14446 for direct referral or search the MoSJE website for the latest list of IRCAs.

Outreach and Drop-In Centers (ODICs)

ODICs are community-based facilities for initial contact, screening, assessment, and counseling. They serve as a non-threatening doorway to formal treatment.

üîó Action Step: ODICs are crucial in connecting youth to the right services. Call 14446 for guidance to your nearest appropriate ODIC.

Immediate Guidance & Confidentiality

Call the 14446 helpline for guidance to your nearest appropriate center. For reporting drug trafficking or illegal activity, call the MANAS Helpline 1933.
